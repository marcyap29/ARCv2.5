## Example: Career Change Decision

### Decision Brief: Career Transition

**Decision Context**: Choosing between staying in current role (stability) vs. pursuing entrepreneurial path (autonomy and growth).

**Options**:
- **Option A**: Stay in current role, negotiate for more autonomy
- **Option B**: Leave to start consulting business
- **Option C**: Hybrid: part-time current role + side consulting

**Criteria**:
1. **Financial Security**: Protecting family stability
2. **Autonomy**: Control over schedule and priorities
3. **Growth Potential**: Long-term career trajectory
4. **Risk Tolerance**: Comfort with uncertainty
5. **Values Alignment**: Living in integrity with purpose

**Scorecard**:

| Option | Becoming Alignment | Practical Viability | Rationale |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Stay (A)** | 4/10 | 9/10 | High security, low autonomy/growth |
| **Leave (B)** | 9/10 | 5/10 | High alignment, high risk |
| **Hybrid (C)** | 7/10 | 7/10 | Balanced but requires discipline |

**Synthesis**:
**Recommended Path**: Hybrid (Option C)

**Why**: Best honors Becoming trajectory (autonomy + growth) while maintaining practical stability during transition.

**Trade-offs**: 
- Gain: Autonomy, growth potential, reduced risk
- Risk: Requires strong boundaries and time management

**Next Steps**:
1. Draft consulting proposal and initial client pipeline
2. Negotiate part-time arrangement with current employer
3. Set 6-month review checkpoint to reassess
